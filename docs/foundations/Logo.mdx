# OCHA Logo

The OCHA logo is our visual signature and the cornerstone of a unified visual identity. It is a **wordmark** incorporating the UN globe-and-wreath emblem and the word "OCHA".

---

## Versions

The logo comes in **4 versions** in blue and white. The horizontal logo is recommended for most information products.

| Version | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Horizontal** (default) | Spaces with limited height or when horizontal alignment complements the layout. Ideal for narrow, wide spaces. |
| **Vertical** | When vertical space is limited or a compact, centred alignment is more effective. Layouts with more height than width. |
| **Spelled-out horizontal** | When the organisation may not be immediately recognised by its acronym. Ensure logo size is sufficient for text readability. |
| **Spelled-out vertical** | Same as above, for vertical contexts. Only works at medium and large sizes. |

---

## Languages

The OCHA logo is available in all six UN official languages: **Arabic, Chinese, English, French, Russian, and Spanish**.

> **Always** use the official acronym **OCHA** as is, without translation. Only translate the spelled-out text where applicable.

---

## Minimum sizes

To guarantee legibility in print and digital media, respect these minimums:

| Version | Print | Digital |
|---|---|---|
| **Horizontal** | 20 mm | **57 px** |
| **Vertical** | 10 mm | **28 px** |

```css
/* CSS tokens */
--cd-logo-min-width-horizontal: 57px;
--cd-logo-min-width-vertical: 28px;
--brand-logo-mobile-width: 57px;    /* enforces minimum */
--brand-logo-desktop-width: 186px;
```

---

## Clear space

A minimum amount of clear space must surround the logo, with no other visual element or type infringing.

**Clear space = half the diameter of the UN globe emblem** on all sides.

```css
--cd-logo-clear-space-ratio: 0.5; /* 0.5 x globe diameter */
```

---

## Background rules

| Background | Logo version |
|---|---|
| White or light neutral (light grey, light brown, etc.) | **Blue (positive)** logo |
| Dark (solid colour or simple dark image) | **White (negative)** logo |

Always ensure enough contrast between the logo and its background.

---

## Do's and Don'ts

### One OCHA

The logo represents the entire organisation as well as individual offices. **Do not** add office names to the logo. Add them to the product's header or footer in an area separate from the logo.

> No "OCHA Afghanistan". No "OCHA Regional Office for Asia-Pacific".

### Never alter the logo

The OCHA logo must **not** be:
- Altered, distorted, or modified in any way
- Recoloured outside of the official blue/white pair
- Outlined, shadowed, or given graphic treatments
- Translated in the acronym (only the spelled-out tagline may translate)
- Placed on a busy image or background with low contrast
- Resized disproportionately (always maintain aspect ratio)

---

## Pairing with other logos

When pairing with logos of other entities:
1. First match the **orientation** (horizontal or vertical)
2. Then match the **size of the UN emblem** (or equivalent emblem on the other logo)
3. If that makes one logo too large or small, match the **size of the type** instead

For further assistance, contact the Design and Multimedia Unit: [ochavisual@un.org](mailto:ochavisual@un.org)

---

## Legal — UN Emblem restriction

Use and display of the United Nations Emblem and OCHA logo is **highly restricted and essentially limited to the organisation's activities**.

- **UN System Entities** (offices, departments, funds, programmes) do not need written authorisation for official use
- **Entities outside the UN System** need permission via the Office of Legal Affairs (gld@un.org)
- **Suppliers** may not use the UN name/emblem to advertise a contractual relationship

Reference: UN General Assembly resolution 92(1) 1946.
